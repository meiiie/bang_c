# Winning Playbooks from Comparable Offline-LLM MCQ Competitions — Cited Effect Sizes + Transplant Plan

## (a) Kaggle "LLM Science Exam" 2023 (2,665 teams, MAP@3, offline, science MCQ — the closest analog to your task)

**1st place — Team H2O LLM Studio** ([writeup](https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/kaggle-llm-science-exam/writeups/team-h2o-llm-studio-1st-place-solution), private 0.933; single best model 0.932):
- **Retrieval**: 3 Wikipedia corpora in parallel (public dump + recreated newer snapshot + **Cirrussearch dump** = fully rendered pages with templates expanded — critical because infobox/math content is missing from plain dumps). Chunked at 256/512/1024 chars preserving sentence boundaries; **512-char chunks best standalone**. 30–60M chunks total. Embedded "article title + chunk" with 5 MTEB models (e5-base/large-v2, gte-base/large, bge-large) for retrieval diversity; brute-force GPU matmul search (no faiss needed).
- **Query format**: question + all options concatenated ("{prompt} {A} {B} {C} {D} {E}"), plus a variant searching per-option and sorting by similarity.
- **Context injection**: train with 3 chunks, infer with 5 (more context at test time helped); **most-relevant chunk closest to the question — inverting context order significantly degraded scores**.
- **Scoring head**: per-option *binary* scoring (context+question forward pass cached as `past_key_values`, then 5 cheap option passes) — chosen explicitly because multi-class scoring "suffered from severe positional bias". Cross-option signal added by feeding the *average* next-token logits of other options into the head.
- **TTA verdict**: they *tried* answer-order-permutation TTA and found it "more shaky and could not outcompete the binary model" — i.e., structurally position-free per-option scoring beat permutation averaging.
- **Ensemble**: simple average of 6 models (5×7B + 1×13B), each with a *different retrieval configuration* — diversity came from retrieval, not model family. Runtime ~9h: 1/3 retrieval, 2/3 inference.

**2nd place (solokin)** ([summary](https://hippocampus-garden.com/kaggle_llm/)): single wiki dump (graelo/20230601.en), overlapping sentence chunks, **BM25 (Lucene)** retrieval + DeBERTa-v3 reranker before prompt assembly; DeBERTa-v3 + Mistral multi-class heads blended with XGBRanker. Lesson: lexical BM25 + neural rerank matched massive dense setups.

**3rd place (podpall)** ([writeup](https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/kaggle-llm-science-exam/writeups/podpall-3rd-place-solution-update-code-links)) — the only one with a public per-component ablation (private MAP@3 deltas):
- **Tuned reranker: +0.0171 — the single largest component**;
- bge-small retriever +0.0012, MiniLM-L6-v2 +0.0026, Xwin-70B add +0.0012, **Platypus2-70B add −0.0004** (a 70B LLM added *nothing* over the tuned retrieval + small-model ensemble);
- their stated conclusion: "the weakest part of the solution is the initial retrieval."

**Baseline deltas showing retrieval is the giant lever**: zero-shot Platypus2-70B + wiki RAG, no fine-tuning = **0.836 public** ([discussion 440620](https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/kaggle-llm-science-exam/discussion/440620), scored by "yes"-token logit per option); public DeBERTa notebooks without proper retrieval sat at ~0.70–0.74 ([example](https://www.kaggle.com/code/alvinleenh/0-701-llm-science-exam-peft-with-deberta)) while the same class of model with the "270K wiki-STEM + faiss" openbook jumped to ~0.86–0.90. Net: **retrieval ≈ +10–15pp MAP@3; everything else (ensembling, rerank, heads) fights over the last ~3–5pp** up to the 0.933 ceiling.

## (b) Math/EDU Kaggle competitions — test-time tricks with measured gains

**AIMO Progress Prize 2 winner (NVIDIA, 34/50)** — [paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.16891), numbers extracted from full text:
- **Quantization ablation (4×L4, maj@12, AIME24/25)**: BF16 82.7/66.7 @210 tok/s; **W8A16 82.7/66.7 @315; FP8 83.3/68.7 @310; W4A16 int4 collapsed to 72.7/60.7** — i.e., **naive 4-bit post-training quantization cost ~10pp accuracy**; 8-bit/FP8 was free speed. FP8+ReDrafter speculative decoding: 554 tok/s (2.6× BF16), 81.3/71.3.
- **Voting + early stop**: temp≈0 "almost greedy" with engine-level nondeterminism providing diversity; maj@12–16 per question; **in a batch of 16, once the first 4–5 completions agreed they cancelled the rest**; straggler cancellation ("shorter answers tended to be higher quality").
- **Time banking**: 350s base per question; unused time banked; next question may draw +210s (max 560s).
- **CoT/TIR checkpoint merging**: CoT 62.9 → TIR 66.8 → linear-merged 0.3·CoT+0.7·TIR **69.1 maj@16** (+6.2pp over CoT) while *shortening* solutions (12.5k vs 15.8k tokens).
- **GenSelect** (LLM reads 16 candidate summaries and picks one, repeated 64×, majority over selections): consistently above plain majority in their Fig. 2 and lifts maj@64 totals by several pp (e.g., 14B 74.6 → 76.6 with self-GenSelect); not used in the Kaggle run due to time.
- AIMO-1 winner (Numina, 29/50): DeepSeekMath-7B two-stage SFT, **SC-TIR with N=48 candidates, 4 rounds of code self-correction, majority vote, 8-bit GPTQ** ([HF blog](https://github.com/huggingface/blog/blob/main/winning-aimo-progress-prize.md)).

**AIMO Progress Prize 3 winner (May 2026 — newest comparable result)** ([writeup](https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/ai-mathematical-olympiad-progress-prize-3/writeups/1st-place-solution-for-the-aimo3-competition)): GPT-OSS-120B MoE on one H100; **8-bit KV-cache quantization** to raise parallel generation count; prefix caching of shared system prompt across parallel samples; **entropy-weighted self-consistency** (Shannon-entropy confidence weights replacing plain majority); adaptive runtime scheduling that reduces sampling depth near the time limit; persistent Jupyter sandbox for verification. Their explicit takeaway: "**prompt engineering consistently produced larger gains than several complex optimization techniques**."

**Eedi Mining Misconceptions 1st place** ([writeup](https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/eedi-mining-misconceptions-in-mathematics/writeups/mth-101-1st-place-detailed-solution)) — cleanest per-component private-LB table in any of these comps (MAP@25, cumulative 0.495 → 0.638):
| Component | Gain |
|---|---|
| Pseudo-labeling synthetic data | **+0.044** |
| Few-shot examples in reranker prompt | +0.036 |
| More hard negatives (ratio→24) | +0.021 |
| CoT injected into reranker input | +0.019 |
| Adding 32B ranker stage | +0.010 |
| Adding 72B ranker stage | +0.013 |

Architecture: Qwen2.5-14B retriever (contrastive LoRA, MultipleNegativesRankingLoss, temp 0.01) → 14B pointwise → 32B pointwise → 72B listwise cascade; reranker score = `logit(Yes) − logit(No)`; **AutoAWQ quantization with task-specific calibration data** for all rankers. Note the shape: **data tricks (+0.044, +0.036) beat adding ever-bigger models (+0.010, +0.013)**.

**Theory/method papers backing the test-time tricks (for sizing):**
- Self-consistency ([Wang et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.11171)): +17.9pp GSM8K, +11.0 SVAMP, +12.2 AQuA, +6.4 StrategyQA, **+3.9 ARC-challenge** (MCQ-like tasks gain least — calibrate expectations).
- **DeepConf** ([Meta, arXiv 2508.15260](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.15260)): confidence(entropy)-weighted voting + filtering top-10% traces beats plain majority; online early-termination of low-confidence traces cuts generated tokens **43–85%** (up to 84.7% on AIME25 at 99.9% acc with 512 samples) with no accuracy loss — directly the AIMO3 winner's mechanism.
- **Selection bias / permutation debiasing** ([PriDe, ICLR'24 Spotlight](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.03882)): position-permutation accuracy fluctuation (RStd) 2–9pp across 20 LLMs; **cyclic permutation averaging: +4.9pp MMLU / +5.7 ARC / +7.9 CSQA on average (at 4–5× cost; gains skew to weaker models)**; PriDe prior-estimation gets +1.2–1.7pp at only 1.15× cost.
- **Efficient permutation voting** ([arXiv 2511.21709, Nov 2025](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.21709)): majority voting over option permutations gains +3.5pp (Qwen/ARC) up to +30pp (Phi-2/QASC, weak-model case), ~1–2pp on MedMCQA; their **BaQCKV trick — cache the Question⊕Context KV once and re-decode only the shuffled options — saves 36–54% tokens / ~84–89% inference time** on the permutation ensemble.
- **Medprompt** ([Microsoft](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.16452)): ablation on MedQA — CoT +3.4pp, dynamic few-shot +2.2pp, **choice-shuffling ensemble +2.2pp** (total +9pp to 90.2%).

## (c) Vietnamese/SEA MCQ competitions

- **VLSP 2025 LegalSLM** ([overview paper](https://aclanthology.org/2025.vlsp-1.21.pdf), 10 teams, ≤4B models, 800 Vietnamese legal MCQs): MC accuracy ranged **74.8% → 92.67%** (winner Bosch@AI Team). Organizers' own ablation: **continued pretraining of Qwen3-4B on 96k Vietnamese legal docs lifted MC 82.1 → 85.0 (+2.9pp)** and syllogism +7.9pp; the same recipe on 1.7B *hurt* (−0.66 MC) — domain pretraining only pays above a capacity floor. A 4B ceiling of 92.67% on Vietnamese law MCQ strongly suggests your 26B+9B stack can clear 93 on a mixed exam.
- **VLSP 2025 MLQA-TSR** (multimodal legal QA, [arXiv 2510.20381](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.20381)): top teams = ensemble of two open MLLMs + structured retrieval over the legal corpus merged into one prompt — again retrieval + small-ensemble, no exotic tricks.
- **Zalo AI Challenge 2023 Elementary Math** ([baseline repo](https://github.com/dinhquy-nguyen-1704/ZaloAI2023-Elementary-Math-Solving), [Reasoning-Lab](https://github.com/Reasoning-Lab/Elementary-Math-Solving-Zalo-AI-2023)): fine-tuned DeepSeekMath ~0.80 private; top approaches = continual pretraining on Vietnamese math corpus + few-shot retrieval of similar solved questions (embedding-kNN over train set) — "dynamic few-shot" à la Medprompt worked in Vietnamese.

## (d) What separates 1st from 10th — converging threads

1. **Retrieval/knowledge quality is the order-of-magnitude lever** (+10–15pp in LLM Science Exam); rerank quality is the largest single sub-component (+0.017 when everything else is tuned); corpus *coverage* (Cirrussearch rendered dumps, multiple snapshots) beats embedding-model choice.
2. **Aggregation refinement gives the last 1–3pp**: weighted voting > plain majority (DeepConf/AIMO3); generative selection > majority (GenSelect); per-option logit scoring > free-form letter generation (H2O 1st, 0.836 zero-shot proof).
3. **Throughput engineering is accuracy**: every AIMO winner spent most effort on tok/s (8-bit/FP8 quant, speculative decode, prefix/KV caching, early stopping) because samples→votes→accuracy. The one catastrophic anti-pattern: **naive int4 PTQ (−10pp)**.
4. **Diversity of evidence, not of decoders**: H2O ensembled 6 near-identical models with *different retrieval configs*; 3rd place showed adding a 70B was worth −0.0004.
5. **Time banking + difficulty-adaptive budgets** (AIMO-2's 350s+210s buffer; 5th-place Science Exam's easy→7B / hard→70B routing) — you score time points without losing accuracy.

Inject only for knowledge domains (civics/law/history/literature/economics); route math/physics/chem past RAG.
2. **Switch final aggregation from plain majority to entropy/confidence-weighted voting with online early-stop** (+0.5–1.5pp and large token savings; DeepConf: −43–85% tokens, AIMO3 winner used exactly this). llama-cpp-python exposes logprobs — compute mean token-logprob (or bottom-decile "tail confidence") per trace, drop the bottom ~50–90%, weight votes by confidence. Keep your agreement gate but adopt AIMO-2's concrete trigger: in a batch of 16 parallel traces, **stop at 4–5 agreeing**; cancel stragglers.
3. **Add a per-option logit-scoring head as a second, position-free signal** (+1–2pp on ties; H2O 1st structural lesson + 0.836 zero-shot recipe + Medprompt +2.2pp shuffling): one prompt per option — "Context… Question… Proposed answer: {option}. Is this correct? Yes/No" — score `logit(Yes)−logit(No)` (Eedi 1st's exact scoring function), with the context+question KV prefix cached so the 4–10 option passes are nearly free (BaQCKV: 36–54% token savings on exactly this pattern — and it makes your cyclic-permutation debiasing ~5× cheaper, since only option order changes). Use it to (a) arbitrate when Gemma/Qwen CoT votes disagree, (b) replace CoT entirely for easy/short factual items to win time points.
4. **Tool-integrated reasoning for the math/physics/chemistry slice** (+0.5–1.5pp overall given the slice share; AIMO-2: CoT→merged-TIR +6.2pp maj@16; all three AIMO winners used code execution): let Qwen3.5-9B emit Python, exec in the Docker sandbox (offline-safe), feed results back, vote over TIR traces. Numina's loop: up to 4 self-correction rounds on traceback.
5. **Disagreement-only GenSelect judge pass** (+0.5–1pp; AIMO-2 Fig. 2 shows GenSelect > majority at equal samples): when weighted voting is still split, show Gemma-26B the 4–8 candidate answers+one-line rationales and have it pick, 3–5 times, majority over picks. Cheap, only touches the hard ~15% of questions.
6. **Quantization audit** (risk-avoidance worth up to 10pp): Gemma-4 QAT Q4_0 is safe (QAT is exactly the mitigation for the AIMO-2 int4 collapse), but **do not run Qwen3.5-9B at naive Q4**; at 9B you can afford Q8_0/Q6_K — AIMO-2 showed 8-bit is accuracy-free speed. Re-benchmark your Qwen quant level on your dev set before finals.
7. **Time banking for the 10 time points** (free; AIMO-2 scheme): per-question base budget = 0.8 × (total/2000), bank surplus, let flagged-hard questions draw up to ~1.6×; near deadline, degrade to single greedy + per-option logits (your 87.26 fallback).
8. **Stretch only if rules permit fine-tuning of allowed models**: LoRA Qwen3.5-9B on synthetic Vietnamese MCQs generated by Gemma-26B with retrieved contexts (H2O trained on RAG-retrieved, not gold, contexts — it worked *better*; Eedi: pseudo-labeled synthetic = their single biggest gain +0.044). This is the 1st-place pattern in every retrieval-based comp, but it's the highest-effort item.

**Anti-patterns to skip** (measured): adding a bigger third model (−0.0004 in Science Exam 3rd-place ablation); full-permutation TTA on top of position-free per-option scoring (H2O found it strictly worse); naive int4 PTQ (−10pp); spending samples uniformly instead of escalating only on disagreement (your existing tiering is already the right shape — AIMO-2/5th-place routing confirms it).

# Offline RAG for Closed-Book MCQ — Deep Research Report

**Verdict up front: CONDITIONAL GO.** Build a ~3-5 GB gated hybrid-retrieval branch (BM25 + BGE-M3 dense over filtered vi-Wikipedia + VN legal corpus), wired into your existing agreement-gated escalation as an *additional voter* — never as a replacement for the closed-book path, and never for math. Expected +1.5 to +4 pts on your 87.26 baseline (medium confidence), with near-zero downside if gated on disagreement. Blanket always-RAG is a NO: it measurably *reduces* accuracy on questions the model already knows.

---

## (a) Effect sizes: when retrieval helps vs hurts

**Kaggle LLM Science Exam 2023 (5-choice STEM MCQ, MAP@3) — the strongest "FOR" evidence:**
- All top solutions were RAG; nobody won closed-book. 1st place (H2O) used multiple Wikipedia dumps + MTEB embedding models + an ensemble of five 7B + one 13B; 2nd place used pure **BM25 (Lucene)** over `graelo/wikipedia 20230601.en` sentence-overlap chunks + DeBERTa reranker ([Hippocampus Garden competition report](https://hippocampus-garden.com/kaggle_llm/), [ML Contests 2023 review](https://mlcontests.com/state-of-competitive-machine-learning-2023/)).
- Concrete retrieval deltas from community writeups: LongFormer + TF-IDF over the shared 270K wiki-STEM corpus → **public LB 0.862**; 270K corpus + faiss → **LB 0.8728** ([silver-medal writeup](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/our-team-got-silver-medal-kaggle-llm-competition-ivan-isaev--lszce), [490CAD silver solution](https://github.com/490CAD/LLM4Science)); a 10th-place team expanded retrieval from 270K to ~6M rows and hit **public 0.930 / private 0.923** ([Lizhecheng02 repo](https://github.com/Lizhecheng02/Kaggle-LLM_Science_Exam)). Community no-retrieval fine-tuned baselines plateaued around 0.75–0.80 (medium confidence — secondary sources); i.e., retrieval was worth roughly **+10–15 MAP@3 points** on knowledge MCQ, the single largest lever in the competition.

**Mallen et al., PopQA ("When Not to Trust Language Models", ACL 2023) — the popularity law ([ar5iv full text](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2212.10511)):**
- GPT-3 davinci-003 closed-book: 35% overall, **19% on the 4,000 least-popular entities**. Contriever retrieval: **+7% overall**, and retrieval-augmented GPT-neo **2.7B beats vanilla davinci-003 (175B)** on tail entities — retrieval is worth orders of magnitude of parameters on long-tail facts.
- But for **~10% of questions retrieval is actively harmful** (recall@1 of 0.14 on those), concentrated on popular entities the model already knows. Adaptive (popularity-gated) retrieval: best accuracy **46.5%** while **avoiding ~40% of retrieval calls** and cutting cost ~50%.

**TARG (Nov 2025, training-free gating) — the strongest "AGAINST blanket RAG" evidence ([arXiv 2511.09803](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09803v2)):** with Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, **Always-RAG is worse than Never-RAG** on TriviaQA (57.6 vs 60.8 EM), NQ-Open (37.4 vs 38.8), PopQA (14.6 vs 20.0). Margin-based gating beats both (TriviaQA 62.2 EM at 33.8% retrieval rate), cutting retrieval calls 70–90%. Your tiered agreement-gating is exactly this mechanism.

**MedRAG/MIRAGE (medical MCQ benchmark) — model-strength interaction ([arXiv 2402.13178](https://arxiv.org/html/2402.13178)):** RAG vs CoT on exam-style MCQ: GPT-3.5 **+2.57% MMLU-Med, +2.79% MedMCQA, +1.57% MedQA**; Mixtral 8×7B **+8.06% average**; but GPT-4 **−2.2% / −3.2% / −1.2%** on the same exam sets. Pattern: mid-size models (your regime — Gemma-4 26B MoE/A4B, Qwen3.5 ≤9B) gain; frontier models with stronger parametric knowledge lose. Best retriever setup was hybrid/fusion (RRF-4, 71.57% avg).

**Newest 2026 confirmation:** RAG-X (Mar 2026, [arXiv 2603.03541](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.03541v1)) on 7–9B models: RAG beats zero-shot and long-context on fact QA (Llama-3.1-8B 0.35→0.71; Gemma-2-9B 0.33→0.69), **hybrid BM25+dense at α≈0.5 is optimal**, and 22% of retrieved context is pairwise-redundant (use MMR/dedup). [Reasoning-trace MCQA study (arXiv 2509.10744)](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.10744): chunk-RAG lifts Llama-3.1-8B 81.9→90.0% and SmolLM3-3B 46.6→75.1% on **non-math** MCQ — and the paper explicitly restricts to non-math subsets because retrieval doesn't transfer to calculation questions.

**Where it hurts (consistent across all sources):** math/calculation, pure reading-comprehension (context already in question), and high-popularity facts the model already answers — noise injection costs 1.5–5.4 EM points if you retrieve unconditionally (TARG, MIRAGE-GPT-4, Mallen's 10%).

## (c) Offline embedding + reranker serving

- **BGE-M3**: 1024-dim dense, 8192-token context, 100+ languages incl. Vietnamese, MIT license ([BAAI/bge-m3](https://huggingface.co/BAAI/bge-m3)). GGUF conversions run under `llama-server --embedding` (same binary you already ship): Q4_K_M = **438 MB** ([bbvch-ai/bge-m3-GGUF](https://huggingface.co/bbvch-ai/bge-m3-GGUF)), f16 ≈ 1.2 GB ([pqnet/bge-m3-gguf](https://huggingface.co/pqnet/bge-m3-gguf), [lm-kit/bge-m3-gguf](https://huggingface.co/lm-kit/bge-m3-gguf)). Caveat: llama.cpp serves **dense vectors only** (no sparse/ColBERT heads) — pair with BM25 for the lexical signal instead. Watch the dense-module conversion pitfall (`--sentence-transformers-dense-modules`) noted in [llama.cpp #21112](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/discussions/21112).
- **Qwen3-Reranker**: GGUF works with `llama-server -m Qwen3-Reranker-0.6B-f16.gguf --reranking --pooling rank` exposing `/v1/rerank`; 0.6B f16 ≈ 1.2 GB, 4B ≈ 8 GB ([Mungert/Qwen3-Reranker-0.6B-GGUF](https://huggingface.co/Mungert/Qwen3-Reranker-0.6B-GGUF), [working-GGUF discussion](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3-Reranker-0.6B/discussions/22)). **Critical pitfall:** most community GGUFs are broken — missing `cls.output.weight` → scores like 4.5e-23; use official `convert_hf_to_gguf.py` per the [models.ini guide gist](https://gist.github.com/VooDisss/42bce4eb5c76d3c325633886c5e348ee) and validate scores before shipping.

## (d) Index tech at your scale (~1.5–3M chunks)

- CPU benchmarks at ~1–2.25M vectors ([Bang for the Buck, arXiv 2505.07621](https://arxiv.org/html/2505.07621)): HNSW ~170–360 QPS even at 1536-dim; brute-force full scan ~42–67 QPS at 128-dim/1M. Community tests find **hnswlib faster than faiss-HNSW**, with faiss most balanced overall ([Milvus benchmark discussion](https://github.com/milvus-io/milvus/discussions/4939), [HNSW implementations comparative study](https://www.techrxiv.org/users/922842/articles/1311476-a-comparative-study-of-hnsw-implementations-for-scalable-approximate-nearest-neighbor-search)).
- At 2000 questions total you need ~2000–4000 queries — **even brute-force matmul (numpy/faiss `IndexFlatIP`) is sufficient**: 2M×1024-d fp16 ≈ 4 GB RAM, batched GEMM answers all 2000 queries in seconds (arithmetic estimate). faiss-cpu `HNSW32` or hnswlib (M=16, efC=200) builds 2M vectors in minutes on a modern CPU (estimate; build offline at image-build time anyway). Use faiss SQ8 to halve embedding storage with negligible recall loss. Index choice is a non-issue — don't over-engineer it; time score impact is ~0.
- Zero-embedding fallback: pure BM25 got 2nd place at Kaggle; a `bm25s`/Lucene index over 2M Vietnamese chunks is <1 GB and CPU-only.

## (e) Retrieval→prompt patterns from winners + selective RAG

- **Query formulation**: Kaggle standard = `question + all answer options` concatenated as one query (1st/2nd/community open-book all did variants); 4th place did sentence-wise Elasticsearch retrieval; some used per-option retrieval then merged ([Hippocampus Garden](https://hippocampus-garden.com/kaggle_llm/)). MIRAGE deliberately used question-only and got smaller gains — for MCQ contests **include the options in the query** (options contain the discriminative entities).
- **Context injection**: top-5 articles → top-20–30 sentences, or 10 pages/30 sentences (10th place), reranked (DeBERTa then; **Qwen3-Reranker-0.6B** for you), deduplicated (RAG-X: 22% of top-k is redundant — apply MMR or near-dup filtering), ~1500–2500 tokens prepended before the question.
- **Selective RAG**: gate retrieval on (i) question type — regex/keyword router: digits+"tính/bao nhiêu/phương trình" → math → **no RAG**; law/civics/history/literature lexicon → RAG; and (ii) confidence — your existing self-consistency vote margin is precisely TARG's margin gate (TARG: gating beats Always-RAG by +1.4 to +8.4 EM while retrieving for only ~30% of queries). Fuse as **extra ensemble voters**: closed-book SC votes + RAG-branch votes; RAG can only flip questions where closed-book votes already disagree → downside capped at ~0 by construction (Mallen's adaptive-retrieval logic).

## Go/No-Go + Minimal Viable Architecture

**GO, gated** — your remaining 12.7-pt error budget on Vietnamese civics/law/history/literature is exactly the long-tail-knowledge regime where mid-size models gain +2 to +8 pts from retrieval (MIRAGE Mixtral +8.06%, chunk-RAG +8.1 pts for Llama-3.1-8B, Mallen tail gains, Kaggle +10–15). Realistic net for you with disagreement-gating: **+1.5 to +4 pts** (medium confidence) — necessary but not alone sufficient for 93; it is the largest orthogonal lever you have not yet pulled. **NO-GO for**: always-on RAG (TARG: −1.4 to −5.4), math/reading questions, full en-wiki, and any reranker GGUF you haven't score-validated.

**MVA (~3.4 GB added to the 15 GB image):**
1. **Corpus** (build-time): `20231101.vi` HF parquet, drop articles <500 chars (kills 58% bot stubs) → ~500K articles → ~1.5–2M chunks (300 tokens, 50 overlap, title prepended) + YuITC legal corpus (119K chunks, 214 MB). Text ≈ 1.2 GB.
2. **Indexes**: BM25 (bm25s, syllable-level tokens, ~0.5 GB) + BGE-M3 Q8_0 GGUF (~640 MB) embeddings stored faiss SQ8 `IndexFlatIP` (~1.6 GB for 1.6M×1024d). Hybrid score α=0.5 (RAG-X optimum). Optional: Qwen3-Reranker-0.6B Q8 (+640 MB) reranking top-50→top-10.
3. **Routing**: math/calc detector → skip RAG. Else run your existing closed-book SC tier first; **only on vote disagreement** spawn a RAG branch (query = question+options, top-8 deduped chunks, ~2K tokens) whose samples join the vote pool.
4. **Runtime cost**: ~30% of 2000 questions × (1 embed + 1 matmul + 1 rerank) ≈ minutes total — immaterial to the 10-pt time score.
5. **Validation gate before commit**: ablate on your dev set split by question type; ship only if knowledge-subset gain > total reasoning-subset loss (expect loss ≈ 0 with gating).

# Test-Time Accuracy Techniques for Offline MCQ (late 2025 – June 2026) — Ranked by Expected Gain

Setup assumed: Gemma-4 26B A4B QAT GGUF + Qwen3.5 ≤9B on llama.cpp, baseline 87.26 (greedy CoT), already running self-consistency + permutation debias + tiered escalation + dual-model voting. Verification status flagged per claim.

---

## TIER 1 — Likely largest gains, near-zero runtime cost

### 1. Replace naive QAT Q4_0 GGUF with Unsloth UD-Q4_K_XL (quant-stack fix, not an algorithm)
Naive llama.cpp Q4_0 conversion of the Gemma-4 QAT BF16 checkpoint **loses 15.43 top-1-token accuracy points on exactly your model (26B-A4B: 70.20% → 85.63% recovered by UD-Q4_K_XL)**, and 8.76 pts on 31B; root cause is llama.cpp's F16 scales vs QAT's BF16 scales (naive conversion only 24.77% byte-exact to the QAT lattice vs 99.96% for Unsloth Dynamic). The UD file is also 200MB *smaller* (14.2 vs 14.44GB). Unsloth explicitly states higher-precision quants of the QAT checkpoint *degrade* accuracy — UD-Q4_K_XL is the recommended single file ([Unsloth Gemma-4 QAT docs](https://unsloth.ai/docs/models/gemma-4/qat), [unsloth/gemma-4-26B-A4B-it-qat-GGUF](https://huggingface.co/unsloth/gemma-4-26B-A4B-it-qat-GGUF)). Google's own figures show QAT itself is near-lossless (12B Q4_0 QAT 67.07 vs BF16 67.15 MMLU 5-shot — [Google blog](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/quantization-aware-training-gemma-4/)), so any conversion loss you carry is pure recoverable headroom. **Caveat**: +15.4 is vendor-reported top-1 *token* accuracy, not MCQ accuracy; one independent head-to-head ([zephel01 round 1](https://note.com/zephel01/n/nf3da543dfd95?hl=en)) mildly favors Unsloth but is inconclusive. Expected MCQ gain if you're on a naive/official Q4_0 conversion: plausibly +1–4 pts. Cost: zero. **A/B this on the public LB first — it's the cheapest experiment you have.**

### 2. Audit KV-cache quantization: never quantize Gemma-4 26B A4B's KV cache
If you enabled `-ctk/-ctv q8_0` (common for llama-server parallel slots), this is hurting you: on **Gemma 4 26B A4B specifically, q8_0 KV cache gives KL divergence 0.377 and q4_0 gives KL 1.088 / 68.0% top-1** — the most KV-quant-sensitive model tested; "q8_0 is practically lossless" does not hold for Gemma MoE ([localbench KV-cache benchmark](https://localbench.substack.com/p/kv-cache-quantization-benchmark) — **single-source, medium confidence**). By contrast Qwen3.5's hybrid attention (8/32 full-attention layers) is **lossless at q4_0 KV (BLEU 1.000 across 10 configs)** ([llama.cpp issue #21385](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/issues/21385)). Recommendation: f16 KV for Gemma, quantize KV only on Qwen if VRAM-bound. Cost: VRAM only. If truly VRAM-bound, community TurboQuant (ICLR 2026) `turbo3` cache types hit PPL 6.20 vs 6.19 f16 on Qwen3.5-35B but are experimental ([llama.cpp discussion #20969](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/discussions/20969)).

## TIER 2 — Upgrades to your existing voting stack (logprob-weighted selection)

### 3. DeepConf: confidence-filtered weighted voting + online early stopping (arXiv 2508.15260, Meta)
Compute per-trace confidence from token logprobs (sliding-window "group confidence"; the **lowest-group-confidence** variant flags "moments of confusion"); offline: keep top-10% most-confident traces, then confidence-weighted vote — **DeepSeek-8B AIME25: 87.4% vs 82.3% majority voting (+5.1 pts at equal samples)**; online early-stop kills low-confidence traces mid-generation, cutting tokens **43–85%** while maintaining/improving accuracy (one 8B config: −77.9% tokens, +5.8 pts) ([paper](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2508.15260), [project page](https://jiaweizzhao.github.io/deepconf/), [ArXivIQ analysis](https://arxiviq.substack.com/p/deep-think-with-confidence), [code](https://github.com/facebookresearch/deepconf)). Training-free; needs only top-k logprobs per token, which llama-server returns (`n_probs`/logprobs). The token savings directly buy you time-score points and budget for deeper escalation tiers. **This is the natural upgrade to your agreement-gated escalation: gate on confidence, not just agreement.**

### 4. CISC: P(True) confidence-weighted self-consistency (arXiv 2502.06233, ACL 2025 Findings)
After each sampled CoT, ask "Is this answer correct? (1/0)" and read the logprob of "1"; softmax-normalize within-question; weighted vote. **+1.1% accuracy at budget 10 and 46% cost reduction (P(True) variant), macro-averaged over 9 models × 4 datasets; SC needs 18.6 samples to match CISC at 10** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/html/2502.06233v1), [code](https://github.com/taubenfeld/CISC)). One extra token per trace — trivially cheap in llama.cpp. Newer refinements: **VecCISC** adds reasoning-trace clustering + candidate selection ([arXiv 2605.08070](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08070v1), May 2026); **RISC** (June 2026) reframes selection as ranking with 5 text-only features + a tiny LambdaRank model — **+4.75–6.49% over SC; beats SC@99 samples using only 18 on PopQA** ([arXiv 2606.05054](https://arxiv.org/html/2606.05054)) but needs a labeled training set (VMLU dev / public LB answers can serve).

### 5. Self-certainty Borda fusion (arXiv 2502.18581) — small but free
Self-certainty = KL-from-uniform of the output distribution, computed from logprobs you already have. **Pure self-certainty BoN underperforms majority voting on closed-form answers — do NOT use alone** — but the **Borda hybrid (vote weight (N−r+1)^p, p≈0.3) beats plain self-consistency on every benchmark tested** (Llama-3.1-8B: MATH@64 63.60 vs 63.40; GSM8K@64 91.07 vs 90.99; LiveBench-Math@32 26.47 vs 26.25) ([paper](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2502.18581)). +0.1–1.2 pts, zero extra generation. Stack under #3/#4 as a tie-breaker in your vote fusion.

## TIER 3 — Prompt-space and knowledge-space levers (potentially large, needs local A/B)

### 6. Format grid-search: the cheapest unexploited points
Effect sizes are far bigger than most algorithm papers: **a single character (e.g., delimiter/spacing/label style) shifts MCQ accuracy ±5–15 pts per model** ([arXiv 2510.05152](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.05152)); few-shot formatting spread reaches **76 accuracy points** in pathological cases ([FormatSpread, arXiv 2310.11324](https://arxiv.org/html/2310.11324v2)); option-order sensitivity 13–75% ([arXiv 2308.11483](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.11483) — you've handled order, not base format). Action: grid ~20 variants (option labels `A.`/`(A)`/`A)`, newline layout, instruction wording, 0/2/5-shot) × instruction language {Vietnamese, English} against the public LB or labeled VMLU dev ([VMLU](https://vmlu.ai/), 10,880 Vietnamese MCQs, [ACL 2025 toolkit paper](https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.563/)). On instruction language the evidence is mixed and model-specific: strong multilingual models often answer better fully in the content language, and "Native-Tone Prompting" helps ([OpenReview localization study](https://openreview.net/forum?id=27pOlHjUge), [native-design-bias arXiv 2406.17385](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2406.17385)) — measure, don't assume. For automated label-free search: **SPO** optimizes prompts via pairwise output comparisons at **1.1–5.6% of prior methods' cost, 3 samples per iteration** ([arXiv 2502.06855](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06855)); **Prompt Duel Optimizer** is the newer label-free dueling-bandit variant ([arXiv 2510.13907](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.13907), updated Apr 2026). Given gold-labeled VMLU exists, direct labeled grid-search is simpler and stronger.

### 7. Offline RAG with BGE-m3 over Vietnamese Wikipedia + law/civics corpora
The rules whitelisting BGE-m3/Qwen-Rerank all but announce this. Every winning team in the closest analog (Kaggle LLM Science Exam: offline MCQ, 5 choices, 2,600 teams) used retrieval; 1st place ensembled 5×7B+13B over multi-dump Wikipedia retrieval, 2nd combined BM25 (Lucene) + dense, with retrieval described as the decisive lever ([competition report](https://hippocampus-garden.com/kaggle_llm/)). For your civics/law/history/literature questions (where CoT can't derive the answer), retrieval is the main path past the knowledge ceiling — likely your biggest remaining lever after Tier 1 if errors cluster in factual subjects. Cost: offline index build + ~1 retrieval per question; hybrid BM25+dense, then Qwen-Rerank.

### 8. Conformal option pruning for 4–10-choice questions (CROQ, arXiv 2501.00555)
Calibrate a conformal threshold on a labeled set (VMLU dev), prune options not in the prediction set, re-ask on the reduced set: "CROQ improves accuracy over standard inference, with more pronounced gains when paired with CP-OPT" on MMLU/ToolAlpaca/TruthfulQA ([abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.00555)) — exact deltas not independently verified by me; gains should be largest on your 8–10-option questions. Note plain process-of-elimination *prompting* is NOT well supported: PoE helps zero-shot logical reasoning ([arXiv 2310.15575](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2310.15575)) but PoE-with-CoT consistently underperforms direct selection ([arXiv 2311.07532](https://arxiv.org/html/2311.07532)); reasoning traces already do legitimate elimination naturally ([arXiv 2510.07761](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.07761)).

## TIER 4 — Compute-shaping (mainly buys time-score + escalation budget)

### 9. Thinking-budget control: cap, don't extrapolate; consolidate, don't re-vote
Post-s1 evidence says naive budget forcing ("Wait"-appending, [s1 arXiv 2501.19393](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2501.19393)) is fragile: extra tokens often **overwrite useful partial work, causing accuracy drops as length grows** ([arXiv 2510.21398](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.21398)); accuracy peaks at intermediate budgets (~6–12k tokens) and weak models gain little ([arXiv 2512.19585](https://arxiv.org/html/2512.19585v1)). Same paper's best finding for you: the **"Summary" strategy — generate multiple candidates, then have the model consolidate them into one answer — beats vanilla self-consistency at equal compute, even with thinking disabled**. Use that as your final escalation tier instead of more votes. For Qwen3.5-9B (supports thinking/non-thinking; GPQA-Diamond 81.7, MMLU-Pro 82.5 — beats prior 30B MoE; [Artificial Analysis](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/qwen3-5-small-models)) cap thinking budget — small Qwens burn ~3× the thinking tokens of larger siblings. Also reconsider model roles: Qwen3.5-9B may deserve equal/primary weight on STEM, Gemma on Vietnamese humanities.

### 10. Entropy-localized intervention and branching (bigger gains, more engineering)
**MTI**: classifier-free guidance applied only at high-entropy tokens, **+9.28% avg across 6 benchmarks on DeepSeek-R1-7B; +11.25% AIME24 on Ling-mini-2.0**, training-free ([arXiv 2510.13940](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.13940), [code](https://github.com/EnVision-Research/MTI)) — but requires CFG-style dual decoding in llama.cpp (custom plumbing; moderate risk). **Entropy-Gated Branching** forks at high-entropy tokens with verifier pruning ([arXiv 2503.21961](https://www.arxiv.org/pdf/2503.21961v4)) — needs a verifier; lower priority. Related June-2026 line: agentic auto-discovery of TTS strategies reports 5–15% over self-consistency via smarter aggregation/filtering ([arXiv 2605.08083](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.08083)) — its discovered components (confidence filtering, early stopping, non-majority aggregation) are exactly Tier 2 above.

### 11. Speed-only items (protect the 10 time points)
Speculative decoding is distribution-lossless; a systematic benchmark of spec-decode under test-time-scaling workloads shows large speedups ([arXiv 2509.04474](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.04474)) — Qwen3.5-0.8B/2B draft for the 9B. MoE **expert offloading (`--n-cpu-moe`/`-ot`) does not change accuracy** — identical weights, different device; it's purely a speed/VRAM placement question; keep always-active tensors on GPU ([llama.cpp MoE offload guide](https://huggingface.co/blog/Doctor-Shotgun/llamacpp-moe-offload-guide)). One nuance: FlashAttention on/off changes numerics slightly (half2 accumulation), which can flip sampled tokens/expert routing between runs — keep settings identical between calibration and submission ([discussion #20969](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/discussions/20969)).

**The diagnostic pattern**: base open models are weakest at STEM (Llama-3-70B: STEM 13.2pp below SocSci); once CoT/reasoning is added (QwQ), STEM flips to the strongest category (+19.4pp over Llama-3) while **Humanities becomes the floor (9.3pp below STEM)** — CoT cannot reason its way to Vietnamese historical/legal facts. Your setup (greedy CoT, 87.26) is structurally a QwQ-like profile: expect residual errors concentrated in **history/law/literature recall + hardest math**, not mid-tier STEM. Vietnamese fine-tunes close the Humanities gap to ~3pp of STEM — they gain via Vietnamese factual knowledge, which you can substitute with offline RAG. Reference points: Gemma-7B zero-shot 41.90 avg, Vistral-7B (VN fine-tune) 50.07 with SocSci 57.02 vs STEM 43.43 ([VMLU GitHub baselines](https://github.com/ZaloAI-Jaist/VMLU)); SeaLLM-7B-v2 45.74 ([HF card](https://huggingface.co/SeaLLMs/SeaLLM-7B-v2)); Sailor2-20B ≈ Qwen2.5-32B/Gemma2-27B on VMLU ([Sailor2, arXiv 2502.12982](https://arxiv.org/html/2502.12982v1)). Confidence: high (official leaderboard + repo).

## (b) VNHSGE / ViGEText / ViMMRC: per-subject distributions and what moved them

**ViGEText_17to23** (3,722 Qs, VN graduation exams 2017–23; [HF uitnlp/ViGEText_17to23](https://huggingface.co/datasets/uitnlp/ViGEText_17to23); [arXiv 2310.12059](https://arxiv.org/html/2310.12059)) — GPT-4, 5-shot, Vietnamese prompts: Civics 88.17 > History 82.17 > Geography 71.92 > Biology 69.17 > Physics 66.60 > Chemistry 64.20 > **Math 56.44**. **Key effect size: 0-shot→5-shot = +15.43pp average; Math +32.35pp** — few-shot exemplars (in Vietnamese, with answer-letter format demonstrated) are the single most documented mover on Vietnamese exam MCQ.

**VNHSGE** (19,000 MCQs, 9 subjects; [arXiv 2305.12199](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.12199), [repo](https://github.com/Xdao85/VNHSGE)) — ChatGPT vs BingChat (= GPT-4 **with retrieval**): History 56.5%→**88.5% (+32pp)**, Civics 70.5→85.5 (+15pp), Geography 61.5→79.5 (+18pp), Biology 58→69 (+11pp); Chemistry stayed lowest (48→52.8). Retrieval moved Vietnamese social/humanities subjects enormously while barely helping math (58.8→60). This is the strongest published evidence that **offline RAG attacks exactly your residual error bucket**. Confidence: high (official repo numbers).

**ViMMRC 1.0/2.0** (grades 1–12 reading comprehension, 2,783/5,273 Qs): multi-stage transformer 58.81% on 2.0 (+5.34pp over best BERTology) ([arXiv 2303.18162](https://arxiv.org/html/2303.18162)); QLoRA-fine-tuned Gemma-7B hits 87.11% on ViMMRC vs GPT-3.5 82.88% ([arXiv 2503.18062](https://arxiv.org/html/2503.18062v1)) — Gemma-class models handle Vietnamese MRC well when adapted. ViMultiChoice (Feb 2026) reports SotA on ViMMRC 2.0 via joint answer+explanation training, no public effect size in abstract ([arXiv 2602.09961](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.09961)). ViExam (2,548 Qs): text-only 70.60% vs multimodal 61.19%; o3-style thinking model 74.07% vs human avg 66.54% ([arXiv 2508.13680](https://arxiv.org/html/2508.13680v1)).

## (c) Does prompting language matter? Measured deltas

- **English instructions + Vietnamese content (your current setup) is supported for open models**: on ViExam, English prompts over Vietnamese content gave open-source models **+2.90pp** (27.70→30.60) while costing closed models −1.05pp ([arXiv 2508.13680](https://arxiv.org/html/2508.13680v1)).
- **Do NOT translate questions to English**: for Vietnamese QA, full pre-translation scored **−5.8% vs selective pre-translation**, and selective gave 0% over direct inference — translate-then-solve is net-negative for Vietnamese, a medium-resource language; source-language context is preferred for extractive tasks ([arXiv 2502.09331](https://arxiv.org/html/2502.09331v1)).
- Converging evidence from Arabic (low-resource analog): English ("non-native") prompts beat native prompts across 3 models on 12 datasets, **even for the Arabic-centric Jais model**, with better label-format adherence in English ([arXiv 2409.07054](https://arxiv.org/html/2409.07054v1)); En-CoT ≥ Native-CoT in the multilingualism survey ([arXiv 2405.10936](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2405.10936)).
- **Net for you**: keep English meta-instructions + answer-letter schema, keep question/options verbatim Vietnamese, let CoT reason in whichever language emerges, and write **few-shot exemplars in Vietnamese** (region-specific knowledge benefits from native-language demonstrations, per 2502.09331). Expected: small positive (~1–3pp) vs full-Vietnamese prompts; large positive (~5pp+) vs any translate-to-English pipeline. Confidence: medium-high (consistent across 3 independent studies; no Gemma-4-specific measurement exists).

## (e) Tokenizer/diacritics/GGUF pitfalls for Gemma-4/Qwen on llama.cpp — three verified, directly applicable

1. **Your exact model's quantization may be silently broken.** Naive Q4_0 conversion of the Gemma-4 QAT checkpoint achieves only **70.2% top-1 agreement** with the BF16 QAT model for 26B-A4B (F16-vs-BF16 scale mismatch); Unsloth's UD-Q4_K_XL restores **85.6% (+15.4pp agreement, 99.96% vs 24.77% byte-exactness), at 14.2 GB (200 MB smaller)** ([Unsloth Gemma-4 QAT docs](https://unsloth.ai/docs/models/gemma-4/qat), [unsloth/gemma-4-26B-A4B-it-GGUF](https://huggingface.co/unsloth/gemma-4-26B-A4B-it-GGUF)). If your image carries a naive Q4_0, this is a free multi-point accuracy recovery.
2. **Windows + recent llama.cpp builds silently destroy multi-byte UTF-8 output for Gemma-4**: PR #21406 (build b8661+) flipped Gemma-4 GGUF loading from SPM to BPE vocab on Windows, dropping multi-byte UTF-8 as empty strings; fixed in PR #21488 ([llama.cpp #21423](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/issues/21423)). Vietnamese is ~all multi-byte UTF-8 — verify your container's llama.cpp build and check load logs say `vocab type = SPM` for Gemma. Related: `<unused24>`/`<unused49>` token floods on gemma-4-26B-A4B were a llama.cpp eval bug fixed ~b8691+ ([#21321](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/issues/21321), [Unsloth discussion](https://huggingface.co/unsloth/gemma-4-26B-A4B-it-GGUF/discussions/2)) — pin llama.cpp/llama-cpp-python to a build ≥ the fix.
3. **Streaming chunk boundaries split multi-byte UTF-8 codepoints** (one Vietnamese char = 2–4 tokens worth of bytes possible): decode-per-chunk corrupts text or raises `UnicodeDecodeError` ([llama-cpp-python #372](https://github.com/abetlen/llama-cpp-python/issues/372), [llama.cpp #4036](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/issues/4036)). Extract answer letters from the fully accumulated byte buffer, never from streamed fragments. Additionally (standard practice, no single-paper citation): normalize all question text and RAG corpus to **Unicode NFC** before tokenization/embedding — NFD/mixed forms of Vietnamese diacritics ("ế" composed vs decomposed) tokenize and embed differently, degrading both generation conditioning and BGE-m3 retrieval matching.

## The 3 highest-leverage Vietnamese-specific actions

1. **Audit the inference stack before any algorithm work (expected: 0 to +several pp, hours of effort).** Confirm the Gemma-4 26B A4B GGUF is Unsloth UD-Q4_K_XL (not naive Q4_0 — 70.2% vs 85.6% QAT agreement), pin llama.cpp ≥ b8691 (unused-token eval bug) and outside the b8661–b8687 Windows BPE-routing window (silent multi-byte UTF-8 drop = silent Vietnamese corruption), confirm `vocab type = SPM` in load logs, NFC-normalize all inputs, and parse answers from complete detokenized bytes. These are silent, Vietnamese-amplified failure modes in your exact model + runtime.

2. **Ship offline RAG (BGE-m3 + Qwen-Rerank, both contest-allowed) over th1nhng0/vietnamese-legal-documents (CC BY 4.0, 3.99 GB) + wikipedia-vi (CC BY-SA, 1.29M articles), routed to history/civics/law/literature questions.** Evidence: retrieval added +32pp on VNHSGE history and +15pp on civics; RAG added +22pp on Vietnamese legal article prediction (VLegal-Bench); humanities is the documented floor of reasoning-tuned open models on VMLU (QwQ: 71.78 vs 81.11 STEM). This targets precisely where a CoT-equipped model at 87.26 still bleeds. Use cheap keyword/subject classification to invoke RAG only on the social/humanities bucket — math/chemistry showed ~0 retrieval gain, so you protect the time score.

3. **Add 5-shot subject-matched Vietnamese exemplars (with explicit answer-letter format) while keeping English meta-instructions; never translate questions to English.** Effect sizes: +15.43pp average (math +32.35pp) from 0→5-shot on Vietnamese graduation-exam MCQ (ViGEText); few-shot > zero-shot consistently on VLegal-Bench; English instructions +2.90pp for open models on Vietnamese exam content (ViExam) — but full translate-to-English pipelines cost −5.8% on Vietnamese (Beyond English). Build per-subject exemplar pools from VMLU-dev (MIT) + VLEMCQ (Apache 2.0) + ViGEText, selected offline by BGE-m3 similarity; this stacks with your existing self-consistency/permutation machinery since exemplars shift the per-sample prior that voting then sharpens.
